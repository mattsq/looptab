**⚠ Load-bearing confound on the "it's the mixing ARCHITECTURE" attribution (adversarial review).** Both mixer arms have TWO advantages over the flat/ff arms at once: (i) cross-cell token-MIXING, and (ii) a per-cell SHARED readout `Linear(latent, H)` (mixer.py), whereas trm_flat/ff_matched carry an UNSHARED `M×H` answer/readout (trm.py) that balloons with horizon and, at fixed budget, starves their width. ## Reading

1. **The mixer's MSE win is horizon-ROBUST; on weather it also GROWS with horizon (direction-robust, magnitude outlier-inflated).** Mixer beats the MLP in the mean at every cell. On weather the margin grows, and the growth survives median/trimming in DIRECTION (median Δ(mixer−ff) −0.19→−0.31→−0.47; drop-worst-block mean −0.28→−0.32→−0.51) — but the raw MEAN progression −0.34→−0.45→−0.99 is ~2× inflated at h720 by ONE hard block (block 1: ff blows up to 6.60, Δ=−5.32; the full per-block Δ vector is [−0.41,−5.32,−1.34,−0.71,−0.05,−0.52,−0.22,−0.37,−0.98,+0.01]). On **etth1 the mean is NON-monotonic** (−0.152→−0.115→−0.184), so "amplifies" is a **weather-only** claim, not both-dataset. The defensible statement: the M26 positive does not decay with horizon; on weather it strengthens.

2. **Per-block SIGN significance splits by dataset.** On weather the effect is large enough to stay sign-significant at every horizon (1/9, p=.021). On etth1 the margin is smaller and cross-block variance grows with horizon (std 0.14→0.29), so only h192 is sign-significant (0/10); h336/h720 are robust-in-mean but not by sign (2/8, 3/7). So: significant on weather-all + etth1-h192; mean-only on etth1-long. (Sign-test p stays INDICATIVE — nested train blocks, Dietterich 1998.)

3. **"It's the architecture, not recurrence" holds; the tied loop is NOISIER at long H (but the "tying reverses" reading is NOT significant).** The non-recurrent untied mixer is the CLEANEST long-horizon signal — Δ(untied_mixer_matched−ff) is 0/10 p=.002 at etth1 h720 (where the *tied* mixer−ff is only 3/7 ns) and −0.96 at weather h720 — because the tied mixer picks up 2 high-variance blocks there, not because untied is fundamentally better. The tying edge Δ(trm_mixer−untied_mixer_matched) is a TIE everywhere (etth1 h720 +0.074 is 7/3, **p=.34 — ns**, a single noisy cell; weather h720 is still −0.032, favouring the *tied* mixer). So the honest read is "tying adds parameter-efficiency but no capability, and its edge is swamped by block variance at long H" — NOT a demonstrated reversal. Consistent with M21/M24e "dressed-up depth," not stronger than at h24.

4. **The deeper long-horizon story: CROSS-VARIABLE modeling vs the independent MLP, via GENERALIZATION (the robust part).** The shallow joint MLP OVERFITS progressively worse as horizon grows — at weather h720 it fits TRAIN best (mse 0.135) yet its test mse exceeds naive persistence on **10/10 backtest blocks** (median test ~1.15, mean 1.646 inflated by the block-1 6.60 outlier; either way, robustly worse than persistence 0.758). Every cross-variable arm generalizes (mixer 0.654, untied 0.686, flat 0.723). Even the flat channel-dependent loop beats ff by −0.923 (0/10, p=.002). So at long horizon the decisive axis is *any* cross-variable inductive bias vs the independent-feature MLP; the mixer is best but the mixer−flat gap narrows (−0.09→−0.07). This is the M26 "fits worse, tests better" mechanism — the most robust finding here (per-block, not outlier-driven). **NB the confound (abstract):** part of "cross-variable" advantage is the shared readout, unseparated from token-mixing.

5. **CD>CI persists in DIRECTION but LOSES M26's significance; the "CI destabilizes" reading is one training divergence and is NOT established.** Δ(flat−decoupled) stays negative (coupling better) in the mean at every etth1+weather cell, but the M26 significance is gone: weather was 9/1 p=.021 at h24, now 2/8 (ns) at h192/h336. The one dramatic number — `trm_decoupled` mse 4437 at weather h720 — is a single **training** divergence (train mse ~91000 on block 9 of 10; every other block/config is stable), i.e. plausibly an LR/seed optimization artifact, NOT demonstrated intrinsic channel-independent instability. So the honest read is "CD ≥ CI in direction at this scale, weaker than M26, with one CI training blow-up" — do NOT read it as "CI destabilizes at long horizon." **Caveat (M26 scope-limit iii stands):** tiny CPU models; the benchmark scale where channel-independence is known to win is far larger, so this bounds nothing there.

## Verdict

The M26 mixer-forecasting positive is **horizon-robust**: the cross-variable mixer beats a param-matched MLP on forecast MSE in the mean at horizons 24→720 on both `etth1` and `weather` (sign-significant on all three weather horizons, mean-only on etth1-long). On **weather it also grows** with horizon — direction-robust by median (−0.19→−0.31→−0.47) though the raw-mean magnitude (−0.99 at h720) is ~2× inflated by one hard block; on etth1 the mean is non-monotonic, so amplification is weather-only. The most robust attribution is **generalization**: the joint MLP overfits progressively worse (worse than persistence on 10/10 weather-h720 blocks) while every cross-variable arm holds. The win is primarily the mixing **architecture** not recurrence (untied mixer carries it; tying is a swamped tie, not a reversal) — **but this is confounded with shared-readout parameter-efficiency, unseparated in this sweep** (the abstract's ⚠ caveat; a shared-readout non-mixing MLP is the needed next control). CD>CI persists in direction but loses M26's significance, and the one CI divergence is a training artifact, not demonstrated CI instability — so the DLinear/PatchTST "CI wins at long horizon" pattern is neither reproduced nor cleanly refuted here. **Scope limits (carry M26's):** (i) two datasets, tiny CPU models — the CD>CI reading may not survive at benchmark scale (§11.3 "benchmark-scale" frontier open); (ii) the paired sign-test p stays INDICATIVE — backtest train sets are nested/overlapping (Dietterich 1998), so the exact binomial is anti-conservative; the story is sign-consistency + median/trimmed growth, not a single p or a single hard-block mean; (iii) one lookback (96), 25 epochs, standardized MSE; (iv) the shared-readout/token-mixing confound above. Rigor: 6 configs × 10 disjoint backtest blocks, all budget-clean (mixer re-widened per horizon, matched-arm ratios 0.985–1.007), additive code (M0–M25 bit-identical), determinism-tested both datasets, generalization verified per-block (train vs test MSE). Canonical: `m30_{etth1,weather}_h{192,336,720}_*`; runner + full run log in `scratchpad/m30/`.
