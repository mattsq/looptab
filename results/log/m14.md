`make_hopfield`/`_build_hopfield_weights` (all-integer ‚áí still bit-exact; the PSD-guaranteeing ő≥ is
derived from the *masked* W so convergence holds). 131 tests (4 new bandwidth tests), ruff clean.

  convergence AND low triviality (b=1 ~84% identity or non-convergent; b=2 ~15% trivial), so w=48 was
  chosen for cleaner margins (not because a local regime is strictly impossible at w‚Č§32).
- **w=48 does:** a single **ő≥=10** gives bandwidth {2,4,8} all 10/10-convergent, balanced (~0.50),

**Reading (per ¬ß8 ‚ÄĒ the locality hypothesis FAILS to revive the edge; mechanistic attribution is a hypothesis, not isolated).**
- **Locality does NOT revive the loop's edge ‚ÄĒ if anything the reverse.** őĒ(loop‚ąíff) is **negative at
  every bandwidth** on accuracy (all 0/10, p=.002); `ff_matched` is the **best arm across the entire
  ladder**. The direct test of M13's hypothesis (does locality revive the *joint-state coherence
  mechanism*?) is the trainability-clean őĒ(stepDS‚ąídecoupled_stepDS), which is **null at both the local
  and dense ends** (ns) and tiny in between ‚ÄĒ nothing like the ECA's +0.32‚Ä¶+0.66. So a *local, non-CA*
  per-cell map does not bring the mechanism back: clean null on the hypothesis as posed.
- **De-emphasise the b=2 őĒEM ‚ąí0.80 ‚ÄĒ it is the LEAST informative cell.** b=2 is an *easy* task (ff acc
  0.999, near-solved) where **both** recurrent arms collapse vs ff (loop őĒEM ‚ąí0.80, decoupled ‚ąí0.94) ‚ÄĒ
  i.e. "recurrence is pointless on an easy task," not a joint-state result. The load-bearing cells are
  the *hard* end (b=8, dense: ff acc 0.92/0.91, EM low for all arms) where the loop **still** fails to
  beat ff and the mechanism delta is **null**. The headline is "no revival even where the task is hard,"
  not the dramatic easy-task number.
- **Why ff tracks bandwidth: per-cell FAN-IN, and the bandwidth‚ÜĒdepth CONFOUND (the key caveat).** ff
  acc runs 0.999 (b=2) ‚Üí 0.906 (dense). A shallow MLP is easy‚áĒeach output cell has small fan-in, and
  fan-in ‚Čą light-cone width ‚Čą **bandwidth √ó convergence-depth**. The banded net changes **two** things
  vs the ECA at once: it drops the *uniform/translation-invariant* rule AND it collapses convergence
  depth (median 1‚Äď2 here vs the ECA's 2‚Äď3 with a tail to ~22). So M14 **cannot cleanly separate**
  "uniform local rule" from "deep convergence / wide light-cone" as the loop-edge ingredient ‚ÄĒ both are
  removed together. The ECA achieves large fan-in via *depth* (radius-1 rule iterated deep); the dense
  Hopfield via *bandwidth* at shallow depth; both are ff-hard, yet the loop won only on the ECA (M13).
  **Hypothesis (NOT isolated by M14):** the loop's edge needs the *iterated translation-invariant local
  rule* specifically ‚ÄĒ but "it's depth-composition, not the uniform rule" remains an equally-supported
  alternative this experiment does not rule out.
- **The tying-positive P1 SURVIVES across a full local‚Üídense ladder.** őĒ(loop‚ąíuntied) is positive and
  significant at **all four bandwidths** (acc 10/0, p=.002 throughout; EM 10/0 at b=2/4/8, 9/1 p=.021 at
