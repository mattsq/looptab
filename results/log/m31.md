# M31 — the M30 shared-readout confound, RESOLVED: the forecasting mixer win is NOT cross-variable MIXING (it's the shared-readout / channel-independent parameterization)

M30 established the mixer-forecasting positive across the horizon axis but flagged a **load-bearing
confound** (its abstract's ⚠, promoted from §11.3 to the named next control): both mixer arms beat
`trm_flat`/`ff_matched` with TWO advantages at once — (i) cross-cell token-**MIXING** and (ii) a
per-cell **SHARED** readout `Linear(latent, H)`, whereas the flat/ff arms carry an **UNSHARED** `M×H`
readout that balloons with horizon (weather-h720: ~983k params ≈ **47%** of `trm_flat`, vs the shared
readout's ~139k with no `M` factor). At fixed budget the unshared readout starves the flat/ff arms'
width, so M30's "it's the mixing ARCHITECTURE" attribution was **equally consistent** with
"shared-readout parameter-efficiency matters more as the readout grows." **No M30 arm separated
them.** M31 adds the one control that does: `trm_mixer_nomix` — a shared-readout, token-mixing-REMOVED
loop (channel-independent), re-widened per horizon to re-match `trm_flat`'s budget. It holds the shared
readout fixed and turns off ONLY mixing, decomposing the M30 headline into two clean legs:

- **Δ(trm_mixer − trm_mixer_nomix) = the token-MIXING operator's contribution.** Both arms are
  budget-matched to `trm_flat` (NOT to each other), so their channel `hidden_dim` differs by ≤1.6%
  (identical only at etth1-h720); this is a clean-ish but not byte-clean mixing isolation — see Caveats.
- **Δ(trm_mixer_nomix − ff_matched) = what the shared-readout / channel-independent parameterization
  buys** on its own.

**Headline: the confound resolves DECISIVELY against the mixing thesis** (the firm claim is the
NEGATIVE one — it is *not* mixing; "harmful" is a weaker, weather-only add-on). Across all 6 cells
(etth1/weather × {192,336,720}, 10 backtest blocks each, all arms within the ±5% budget tol —
the `nomix` control arm at ratios 0.998–1.003, `trm_mixer` at 0.985–1.002):

1. **Token-mixing at a held shared readout is NOT the mechanism — it contributes nothing and is, at
   most, weakly harmful (weather only).** Δ(mixer−nomix) MSE is POSITIVE in the mean in every cell
   (+0.03→+0.16) and sign-significant *against* mixing on all three **weather** cells (10/0, 9/1, 10/0;
   p≤.021), but on **etth1 it is null** (8/2, **5/5 tie**, 8/2). "Harm grows with horizon" is a mean
   artifact: by MEDIAN the mixing penalty is non-monotone in both datasets (weather 0.059→0.141→0.117;
   etth1 0.026→**0.002**→0.089). Defensible claim: remove the cross-variable operator, keep the shared
   readout, and the model does **as well or better** everywhere — decisively so on weather.
2. **The ENTIRE M30 headline is the shared-readout / channel-independent parameterization.**
   Δ(nomix−ff) MSE is strongly negative in every cell (all 0/10–1/9, p≤.021); the non-mixing arm
   captures — and **exceeds** — the mixer's advantage over the joint MLP. The margin grows with horizon
   by MEDIAN on weather (−0.24→−0.43→−0.65), **but the raw-mean progression −0.44→−0.59→−1.13 is ~1.7×
   inflated at h720 by ONE `ff` backtest block that blows up to MSE 6.60** (drop-that-block mean −0.66;
   median −0.65) — the same single-hard-block inflation M30 flagged about its own weather-h720 mean.
   Read the median: the growth is real in direction but far smaller than the mean at h720.
3. **`trm_mixer_nomix` is the single BEST arm in every cell** (lowest MSE everywhere), beating the
   channel-*dependent* mixer AND flat loop AND ff AND persistence. Since it is channel-INDEPENDENT
   (each cell sees only its own variable), this is mild evidence *for* channel-independence at long
   horizon — the DLinear/PatchTST direction M30 could neither reproduce nor refute — and reframes
   M30's "amplification" as the shared-readout/CI advantage amplifying, NOT mixing.
4. **M30 is reproduced to within rounding** (NOT strictly bit-identical). `trm_flat` and `ff_matched`
   are bit-identical to M30 per-seed in EVERY cell; `trm_mixer` matches in 5/6 cells but drifts at
   weather-h720 (MSE 0.656364 vs M30's 0.653787, Δ~2.6e-3) — 3-D-matmul BLAS non-determinism at the
   largest width, despite `num_threads=1` (benign for the verdict, but it means the biggest mixer is
   not strictly bit-reproducible). Δ(mixer−ff) reproduces M30 to rounding (etth1 −0.152/−0.115/−0.184;
   weather −0.343/−0.452/−0.990 vs M30's −0.992). The decomposition is exact (a within-run identity,
   not independent corroboration): mixer−ff = (mixer−nomix) + (nomix−ff) in every cell.

## Reading

1. **Mixing is not the mechanism — readout-sharing / channel-independence is.** M30's #7–8 finding
   (§11.2) credits the cross-cell mixing operator for the forecasting transfer. M31 shows that at a
   HELD shared readout, the mixing operator adds nothing (and, on weather, weakly hurts). The whole
   M30 mixer-over-ff win is the cheap shared readout freeing budget for a wider body plus a
   channel-independent inductive bias. So the correct attribution for the FORECASTING positive is the
   **shared-readout / channel-independent parameterization**, NOT token-mixing. (Which sub-part of that
   package carries it — the readout vs channel-independence vs per-variable weight-sharing — is not yet
   separated; see Caveats. The firm, well-isolated claim is the negative one: it is *not* mixing.)

2. **Why mixing helps on Sudoku/`converge` but does NOT on forecasting (sharpening the §11.2 #8
   dividing line).** The mixer thesis was validated on tasks with genuine, tight cross-cell CONSTRAINT
   coupling (Sudoku Δ EM +0.89; `converge`/`hopfield`/`disruption`), where propagation is the whole
   task. Real forecasting variables are only weakly/noisily coupled, so forcing cross-variable
   information flow at fixed budget is neutral-to-costly (weakly costly on weather) — channel-
   independence wins (the known DLinear/PatchTST result). M31 does NOT overturn the synthetic mixing
   wins; it shows real forecasting is on the CHANNEL-INDEPENDENT side of the line, and the M26/M30
   "win" was never the mixing operator doing cross-variable work.

3. **The horizon "amplification" lives in the shared-readout term, not mixing.** M30's weather
   amplification (−0.34→−0.45→−0.99) is entirely the Δ(nomix−ff) leg (median −0.24→−0.43→−0.65, all
   p≤.021); the mixing leg does NOT amplify by median (weather 0.059→0.141→0.117, non-monotone).
   Mechanically obvious: the unshared M×H readout's budget share grows with horizon, so the
   shared-readout arms' width advantage — and the ff arm's overfit (M30's generalization finding) —
   both amplify. **Honesty note:** the RAW-MEAN version (−0.44→−0.59→−1.13) inherits M30's own
   single-hard-block inflation (one `ff` weather-h720 block at MSE 6.60 doubles the h720 mean); the
   median progression above is the honest, block-robust statement of the growth — direction-robust,
   ~1.7× smaller than the mean at h720. This is M30's amplification mechanism confirmed on the
   shared-readout leg, NOT a cleaner outlier-free version of it.

4. **Channel-independence is the best forecasting arm here.** `trm_mixer_nomix` wins every cell and beats
   persistence at every weather horizon. This is mild, scale-limited evidence FOR the CI direction M30
   flagged (scope-limit iii) — consistent with, though far below the scale of, DLinear/PatchTST. Note
   the M30 CD>CI leg used `trm_decoupled` (a DIFFERENT channel-independent arm — full input per cell,
   decoupled latents); `nomix` is CI in a cleaner sense (own-variable input) and it is the stronger
   arm, so "CD>CI at this scale" (M30 #10 / #8) is further weakened, though not cleanly refuted (the
   arms differ in more than the CD/CI axis).

## Caveats / scope

- **The mixing isolation is clean-ish but NOT byte-clean; the shared-readout isolation is a bundle.**
  Δ(mixer−nomix) varies token-mixing, but the two arms are budget-matched to `trm_flat` (not to each
  other), so their channel `hidden_dim` differs by ≤1.6% (equal only at etth1-h720). In the strongest
  anti-mixing cell (weather-h720, 10/0) `nomix` carries +1.6% MORE params than `mixer` — a small
  capacity edge that flatters the anti-mixing verdict. Mitigant: weather-h336 `nomix` is actually
  NARROWER than `mixer` (1196 vs 1200) yet still wins 9/1, so the weather result is not a pure capacity
  artifact; and the one byte-equal cell (etth1-h720) is the expected direction (8/2) though not
  sign-significant. A perfectly clean mixing leg would match `nomix` to `trm_mixer` — a cheap follow-up
  if the ≤1.6% bothers a reader. Separately, Δ(nomix−ff) bundles THREE things `nomix` has and `ff`
  lacks: the shared readout, channel-independence, and per-variable weight-sharing of the channel MLP.
  So "it's the shared readout" is shorthand for "it's that cheap-parameterization package," not the
  readout matrix in strict isolation. Naming the single active ingredient would need a further control
  (a shared-readout *channel-dependent* MLP, and/or an unshared-readout channel-independent arm). For
  M30's specific claim — "it's the cross-variable MIXING architecture" — the mixing leg already settles
  it (negative in every cell), the ≤1.6% width gap notwithstanding.
- **Recurrence is not the axis here** (as M30/M24e found): this is all standard-train, tied loops; the
  earlier "architecture not recurrence" reads are untouched. M31 only re-attributes the *architecture*
  advantage from mixing to readout-sharing.
- **Carries M30's scope:** 2 datasets, one lookback (96), tiny CPU models, 25 epochs, standardized MSE;
  paired sign-test p is INDICATIVE (nested backtest train sets, Dietterich 1998) — the story is
  sign-consistency across 6 cells (mixing NOT the mechanism) + median amplification of the shared-readout
  leg, not a single p or a single mean. Benchmark-scale (where CI is known to win outright) remains the
  open frontier (§11.3).
- **Reproduction is to rounding, not strictly bit-identical.** `trm_flat`/`ff_matched` are bit-identical
  to M30 per-seed everywhere; `trm_mixer` drifts at weather-h720 only (0.656364 vs 0.653787, ~2.6e-3) —
  3-D-matmul BLAS non-determinism at the largest width despite `num_threads=1`. Benign for the verdict,
  but the biggest mixer is not strictly bit-reproducible; use "to within rounding."
- **Rigor:** 6 lean configs (4 arms: `trm_mixer`, `trm_mixer_nomix`, `trm_flat`, `ff_matched`; M30's two
  untied arms dropped — the nomix control resolves the confound alone), 10 disjoint backtest blocks each,
  all arms within the ±5% budget tol (nomix re-widened per horizon to re-match `trm_flat`, control-arm
  ratios 0.998–1.003; `trm_mixer` 0.985–1.002; verified by a param probe against the real `get_model`
  builder AND the realized `_params.csv`). Additive, off-by-default code (`disable_token_mix=False` ⇒
  `TRMMixer` byte-identical, guarded by a unit test). `trm_mixer/trm_flat/ff` reproduce M30's Δ(mixer−ff)
  to within rounding (per the reproduction caveat above).

## Verdict

The M30 mixer-forecasting positive is **real but MIS-ATTRIBUTED**: it is the per-cell **shared readout /
channel-independent parameterization** (a cheap, weight-tied output param that stops the M×H output layer
from eating the budget as horizon grows) — **not** the cross-cell token-mixing operator, which at a held
shared readout contributes nothing (and, on weather, weakly hurts). The firm, well-isolated claim is the
NEGATIVE one: it is not mixing (sign-consistent in all 6 cells; decisive on weather). The best forecasting
arm is the shared-readout, **channel-INDEPENDENT** loop (`trm_mixer_nomix`), which wins all 6 cells in the
mean and beats persistence at every weather horizon. This resolves the M30 confound (its ⚠ / §11.3 named
control) decisively against the "it's the cross-variable mixing architecture" reading, sharpens the §11.2
#8 dividing line (mixing helps only where outputs are genuinely constraint-coupled; real forecasting is on
the channel-independent side), and locates M30's horizon "amplification" in the shared-readout leg (median
−0.24→−0.43→−0.65 on weather; the −1.13 h720 mean is ~1.7× inflated by one `ff` outlier block — read the
median). Two honesty caveats bound it: the mixing leg is budget-matched to `trm_flat` not `trm_mixer` (≤1.6%
width gap), and "shared readout" is shorthand for a 3-part parameterization bundle (readout + channel-
independence + weight-sharing) not yet separated. Canonical: `m31_{etth1,weather}_h{192,336,720}_*`;
runner + full log in `scratchpad/m31/`.
