**Reading (both instances agree; the story sharpens with variable count):**
1. **The cross-variable mixer beats the shallow MLP on forecast MSE** — the transfer M25's multi-label lacked, and it STRENGTHENS from 7→21 vars (−0.089 9/1 → −0.188 0/10). The difference from M25 is exactly the shared input/output cell topology.
2. **It is primarily the ARCHITECTURE, not recurrence** (the M24e/M24f pattern): a non-recurrent budget-matched untied mixer captures the bulk of the win on both (−0.087 / −0.163), and the tied loop ≈ the 7.9× untied ceiling. **The tying (P1) contribution is width-dependent** — a non-significant tie at 7 vars, a small but significant edge at 21 (−0.025, 0/10) — mirroring M24f/disruption (tying becomes a generalization edge at higher width where the untied stack overfits). So: *a mixing architecture does the work; weight-tied recurrence adds parameter-efficiency that becomes a small generalization edge at scale.*
3. **Plain recurrence does NOT do it** — the flat loop ties ff at 7 vars (+0.008) and only modestly leads at 21 (−0.068, ns); the win needs the cross-variable MIXING operator (mixer − flat −0.097 / −0.120, both sig).
4. **Cross-variable coupling helps (CD > CI), significantly at 21 vars** (leg-1 Δ(flat−decoupled) −0.087, 9/1, p=.021; channel-independent `trm_decoupled` is the WORST arm on both). A partial counterpoint to the DLinear/PatchTST "channel-independence wins" claim — at this tiny scale, cross-variable modeling helps, and explicit mixing helps most.
5. **It is a GENERALIZATION win, not capacity** (adversarial-review-verified): the mixer arms fit TRAIN worse (ETTh1 0.185/0.194 vs ff 0.142; weather 0.105/0.085 vs ff 0.069) yet TEST better, while ff/flat OVERFIT (weather ff train 0.069 → test 0.390, a 5.6× gap). An inductive-bias/regularization benefit of the mixing structure, not more fitting.
